Peking Duck

Description

a famous Chinese dish known for its crispy skin and tender meat, traditionally served with thin pancakes, hoisin sauce, scallions, and cucumber

Ingredients

  • Whole Duck
  • Honey
  • Soy Sauce
  • Five-Spice powder
  • Ginger
  • Garlic
  • Sugar
  • Rice Vinegar
  • water
  • maltose
  • ShaoXing Wine

Steps

  1. Pat dry the duck with kitchen paper to remove any moisture then rub a good layer of fine salt all over (including the cavity). Leave it to rest for about 1 hour over a wire rack inside a tray
  2. Boil some water in the kettle then gently pour it over the entire duck skin
  3. Put Mai Ya Tang, on the surface of the duck. You will need hot water to soften and dilute maltose and add a small amount of vinegar (any type). Evenly brush one layer over the entire duck skin. Leave to dry in the fridge for 1 hour then brush another layer
  4. Put the duck (over a rack and inside a tray) in the fridge without any cover. Keep it there for 24 to 48 hours. You can shorten the process by blowing cool air at the duck with a fan if available
